Vista General

Este juego es un realista bi-dimensional red de masa-resorte, que simula cuerpos fisicos (Por ejemplo Barcos) flotando en el agua. Tu puedes hacerle agujeros a los objetos, cortarlos, aplicar fuerzas a este, y romperlo con explosiones de bombas. Cuando el agua comienza a entrar al objeto, comenzara a hundirse y, si eres lo suficientemente suertudo, comenzara a romperse!

Nuestra filosofia en la creacion de este juego es simular el mundo real de la manera mas precisa. Tu puedes crear tus propios objetos fisicos dibujando imagenes usando los colores que corresponden a los materiales en la libreria del juego. Cada material tiene sus propias caracteristicas fisicas, como la masa, fuerza, rigidez, permeabilidad, caracteristicas sonoras, electricas, etcetera. Si quieres, puedes tambien aplicar una imagen de alta resolucion para ser usada como una textura mas realista para el objeto. Una vez que cargas el objeto, miralo flotar y descubre como soporta el estres! Lee la guia de "Como construir un barco" en al directorio de "Guias" para una explicacion detallada de como construir tus propios objetos.

El juego contiene objetos de ejemplo - Mayormente Barcos. Puedes encontrarlos en el directorio de "Barcos"

Herramientas

Once you load a ship, you can do quite a few things with it.
First of all, you can use the Smash tool to punch holes in it. The longer you keep the mouse button pressed, the larger the hole you can punch! Then there's the Slice tool, with which you can slice your ship.
The Attract tool and the Swirl tool apply radial and tangent forces, respectively. Also with these tools, the longer you keep the mouse button pressed, the stronger the forces become. You can invert the direction of the forces with the key.
The Pin tool allows you to "freeze" a point of the ship: once the point is frozen, its velocity becomes zero and its position never changes. It's best to increase the overall strength of the ship via the Simulator Settings window when you use this tool, or else the frozen point will quickly break under the enormous stress brought by neighboring points. A pin can be removed by applying the tool to the same point again.
With the Move tool you can move (or rotate, if pressing the key) parts of a ship, while the Move All tool allows you to move (or rotate) an entire ship.
With the Pick'n'Pull tool you may pick a particle and move it around - but be careful, if you pull too quick it'll break!
The Flood tool allows you to inject water into a ship, or to extract water from a ship when pressing the key.
The HeatBlaster tool may be used to add heat to parts of the ship, sending them dangerously close to their burning point; pressing the key makes the tool remove heat instead.
After a ship has sunk to the bottom or has burnt for some time, its structure gets rusted and charred; you can use the Scrub tool to clean the dirt away.
The WaveMaker tool may be used to manually generate waves, while the Adjust Terrain tool allows you to customize the ocean floor, creating abysses and islands as you see fit.

Finally, for the passive-aggressive types, ultimate destruction can be achieved lazily with bombs.
Timer Bombs explode automatically a few seconds after having been placed, while Remote Controlled Bombs and Anti-Matter Bombs await for you to detonate them with the and keys, respectively. Impact Bombs just explode on...impact.

If you feel remorse for the destruction you have inflicted, you may try the Repair Structure tool on the broken edges of a ship, and if you are skillful enough, you'll be able to restore the structure of the ship to its pristine condition.

Control de Vision

You can zoom the view with the and keys or with your mouse's wheel, while you can pan the view using the keys. If you get lost, the key resets the view to its default coordinates.
Darkness and light can be adjusted with the and keys.
